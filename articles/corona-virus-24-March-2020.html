Part B – Various Allowances and Grants Available to Small to Medium Enterprises

We are in the process of investigating how these grants work and how to apply for them. Currently there is a link which has been sent to us, whereby we are able to input data on behalf of our clients to obtain the first phase of the grant. We have identified the first set of clients who have contacted us in order to apply for this grant. These clients are already being processed for the grant. Should you not be one of those clients, please contact us immediately should you require any assistance with this compensation application. Some of the allowances which were mentioned are as follows:

1. A special dispensation for companies in distress.

This provides a temporary employment relief scheme to avoid retrenchment of employees; commercial banks exempted from the competition act to allow for corporation.

2. UIF Funds to be extended to workers in SME’s and vulnerable firms and whose companies cannot provide support.

3. Assisting businesses that may be in distress by providing a tax subsidiary of up to R500 per month for employees earning below R6500 per month.

4. Accelerate employment tax incentive reimbursement to twice a year.

5. Tax compliant businesses with a turnover of less than R50 million will be allowed to withhold 20% of PAYE (Employees Tax) and Provisional Tax liabilities without attracting interest or penalties.

6. Temporary reduction to UIF.

7. Small Business Development made R500 million available to distressed SME’s.

8. Industrial Development Corporations R3 billion funding to aid distressed SME’s fighting the virus.

Should you be out the country and unable to run your business, please contact us as we can assist you with this as well.

Part C – Debt Relief Funds set up by the Government

We would like to inform you that if you run or own a small or medium size business that is directly impacted by the shutdown because of Covid-19, please ensure that all your paperwork in order.

The government has set up a debt relief fund to help mitigate losses of small business. If you have payments that are in line with your business there's provision being made. If your business involves, labour, raw materials and operational costs that need money there is business relief.

Should you require this kindly contact us to register you on the website www.smmesa.gov.za when the portal opens on 24 March 2020.
